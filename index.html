Mary E. Stone-Duffy

Mary E. Stone-Duffy, DNP, APRN, PMHNP-BC is a board-certified psychiatric nurse practitioner. Dr. Stone-Duffy received her master’s degree in psychiatric/mental health nursing from Florida International University and a Doctor of Nursing Practice degree from Florida Atlantic University. She has over 35 years’ experience in the field of psychiatric/mental health.

Dr. Stone-Duffy specializes in the diagnoses and treatment of psychiatric disorders including depression, anxiety, bipolar disorder, PTSD and addiction. She has worked in community mental health, substance abuse and in the Veterans Affairs Medical Center. She was also on staff at the Office for Health Services at the United States Peace Corps in Washington, DC. where she was an International Heath Coordinator.

In addition to her clinical responsibilities Dr. Stone-Duffy is an adjunct professor at Florida State University. She has lectured at several local, national, and international conferences about PTSD, moral injury, psychiatric medications, and compassion fatigue. In 2014 Dr. Stone-Duffy was awarded the Clinical Excellence Award at Florida Atlantic University, College of Nursing.

Dr. Stone-Duffy characterizes her role of clinician as one that involves partnership, empathy, and collaboration. She has a deep respect for patients as unique living beings and encourages her patients to become an active participant in their care.
